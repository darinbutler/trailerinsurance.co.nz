4:T665,<p>New Zealand's dramatic landscape comes with natural hazards. From severe storms and flooding to earthquakes and volcanic activity, the risks to property — including trailers and caravans — are real and significant.</p>

<h2>What Natural Events Are Typically Covered?</h2>
<p>Comprehensive trailer insurance generally covers storms and high winds, hail damage, flood and water damage, earthquake damage, lightning strikes, and volcanic activity. However, coverage specifics vary between insurers, so always check your policy wording.</p>

<h2>Flood Cover: Read the Fine Print</h2>
<p>Flood cover is one area where policies differ significantly. Some insurers include flood cover as standard, while others exclude it or charge an additional premium. If you store your trailer in a flood-prone area or travel to regions with flood risk, ensure your policy specifically includes flood protection.</p>

<h2>Preparing Your Trailer for Storm Season</h2>
<p>Reduce your risk by securing your trailer with tie-downs or anchors during storm warnings, parking away from trees and loose objects, ensuring your caravan's roof and seals are in good condition, removing awnings and annexes when severe weather is forecast, and keeping your trailer registration and insurance documents in a safe, accessible place.</p>

<h2>After a Natural Disaster</h2>
