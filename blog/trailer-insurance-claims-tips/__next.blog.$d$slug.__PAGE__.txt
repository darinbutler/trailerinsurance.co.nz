4:T586,<p>Making an insurance claim can feel stressful, but being prepared and knowing the process can help ensure a smooth experience. Here's how to handle a trailer insurance claim in New Zealand.</p>

<h2>Immediate Steps After an Incident</h2>
<p>Ensure everyone's safety first, then secure your trailer if possible to prevent further damage. If theft is involved, contact the police immediately and obtain an event number. Take photos and video of any damage from multiple angles. Note down the date, time, location, and circumstances of the incident.</p>

<h2>Contact Your Insurer Promptly</h2>
<p>Most insurers require you to report a claim as soon as reasonably possible. Have your policy number ready, along with the police event number if applicable, and all documentation you've gathered.</p>

<h2>Document Everything</h2>
<p>The more evidence you can provide, the smoother your claim will be. Keep receipts for your trailer's purchase and any modifications, take regular photos of your trailer's condition, maintain service and maintenance records, and keep receipts for any emergency expenses (towing, storage) after an incident.</p>

<h2>What to Expect During the Process</h2>
<p>Your insurer will assign a claims handler, who may send an assessor to inspect the damage. For straightforward claims, you may receive payment within 10-20 working days.
