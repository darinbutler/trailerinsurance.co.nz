4:T6f6,<p>Trailers are an essential part of the Kiwi lifestyle. Whether you're towing a caravan to the Coromandel, hauling a boat to Lake Taupo, or using a flatbed for weekend projects, your trailer is a valuable asset that deserves proper protection.</p>

<h2>Why Standard Car Insurance Isn't Enough</h2>
<p>Many New Zealanders assume their car insurance automatically covers their trailer. While most comprehensive car policies do extend some cover to trailers, this protection is typically limited to when the trailer is physically attached to the insured vehicle. The moment you unhitch your trailer at a campground, in your driveway, or at a storage facility, your car policy usually provides zero protection.</p>

<h2>What Standalone Trailer Insurance Covers</h2>
<p>A dedicated trailer insurance policy provides comprehensive protection regardless of whether your trailer is hitched or unhitched. Typical coverage includes accidental damage from collisions and impacts, fire and theft protection, storm and natural disaster damage, third-party liability up to $20 million, and optional contents cover for items stored inside.</p>

<h2>Choosing the Right Policy</h2>
<p>When selecting trailer insurance, consider the following: your trailer's value and replacement cost, whether agreed value or market value suits you better, the excess amount you're comfortable paying, any optional extras like contents cover or roadside assistance, and your budget for annual premiums.</p>

<h2>Getting Started</h2>
<p>The best approach is to compare quotes from multiple insurers. Request quotes from at least three providers, compare the coverage levels and exclusions carefully, and don't just choose the cheapest option — consider the overall value and claims reputation of each
