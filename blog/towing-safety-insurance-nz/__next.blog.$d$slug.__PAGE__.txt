4:T635,<p>Towing a trailer on New Zealand roads comes with legal obligations and safety responsibilities. Failing to meet these requirements can not only put lives at risk but could also void your insurance coverage.</p>

<h2>New Zealand Towing Regulations</h2>
<p>Key legal requirements include ensuring your towing vehicle's tow rating exceeds the trailer's gross weight, that the trailer has a current warrant or certificate of fitness if over 2,000kg, that safety chains are properly connected, that all trailer lights and indicators are working, and that the load is properly secured and within weight limits.</p>

<h2>Insurance Implications</h2>
<p>Insurers can decline claims if you were towing illegally at the time of an incident. Common issues include exceeding your vehicle's tow rating, towing without proper safety chains, operating an unwarranted trailer, overloading beyond the trailer's rated capacity, and using incorrect coupling equipment.</p>

<h2>Speed Limits When Towing</h2>
<p>While there is no specific reduced speed limit for towing in New Zealand, the Road Code recommends travelling at reduced speeds when towing, especially in adverse conditions. Excessive speed while towing is a common factor in accidents and can affect your insurance claim.</p>
