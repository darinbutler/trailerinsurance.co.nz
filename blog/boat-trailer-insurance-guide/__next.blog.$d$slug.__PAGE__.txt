4:T65f,<p>New Zealand is a boating paradise, and for most recreational boaties, a good boat trailer is just as important as the boat itself. Yet many boat owners overlook insuring their trailer separately, assuming their marine or car insurance has them covered.</p>

<h2>The Gap in Your Coverage</h2>
<p>Marine insurance covers your boat, and car insurance covers your car — but the trailer often falls through the cracks. Your car policy may only cover the trailer while it's hitched, and your marine policy likely doesn't cover the trailer at all. This leaves your boat trailer exposed to risk at the boat ramp, in your garage, or at a storage yard.</p>

<h2>What Boat Trailer Insurance Covers</h2>
<p>Standalone boat trailer insurance typically covers theft (both hitched and unhitched), accidental damage including launch ramp incidents, storm and flood damage, fire, third-party liability if the trailer causes damage to others, and corrosion damage (on some policies).</p>

<h2>Saltwater Considerations</h2>
<p>Boat trailers face unique risks from saltwater exposure. Corrosion is a major concern, especially for trailers used in the sea. Some insurers specifically cover or exclude saltwater corrosion, so check your policy carefully. Regular maintenance including freshwater rinsing after each saltwater use is essential.</p>
